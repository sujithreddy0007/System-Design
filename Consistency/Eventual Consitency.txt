Eventual Consistency is a consistency model where all replicas may temporarily have different data, but if no new updates occur, they will eventually converge to the same value.

Example

Suppose a user updates their profile photo:

Write goes to Replica A.
Replica B and Replica C are updated asynchronously.
For a short time:
Replica A → New photo
Replica B → Old photo
Replica C → Old photo
After replication completes:
All replicas show the new photo.

This temporary inconsistency is acceptable in many large-scale systems.

Real-world Examples
Social media feeds
DNS
Amazon DynamoDB (configurable)
Cassandra
Shopping cart systems
Why Use It?
Higher availability
Better scalability
Lower latency
Tradeoff
Users may temporarily see stale data.
